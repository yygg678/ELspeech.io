Enhancement of Electrolaryngeal Speech based Speech-to-Speech Conversion using Bottleneck Feature

Authors: Yaogen Yang, Haozhe Zhang, Zexin Cai, Yao Shi, Ming Li

Abstract: Electrolarynx (EL) is a medical device about the size of a small electric razor used to produce clearer speech by those people who have lost their voice. However, the EL speech sounds very unnatural and unintelligible due to the mechanical excitation and its monotonous pitch. To address these issues, we proposed an end-to-end voice conversion method for EL speech enhancement in Mandarin. A speaker-independent automatic speech recognition (ASR) model is used to extract bottleneck features. In the training stage, the bottleneck feature of EL speech is mapped by a parallel non-autoregressive model to the one of the normal speech. Then another model maps normal speech's bottleneck directly to normal speech Mel-spectrogram. In the test stage, the predicted bottleneck from EL speech is fed to the model to produce normal Mel-spectrogram. A MelGAN-based vocoder network is adopted to convert the Mel-spectrogram into time-domain waveform. Experimental results show that the proposed method achieves higher intelligible and mean opinion score(MOS), and it generates state-of-the-art results.
